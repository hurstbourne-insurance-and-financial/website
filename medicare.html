Why Understanding Medicare Matters

Medicare provides valuable healthcare coverage, but it's not all-inclusive. Original Medicare (Parts A and B) leaves gaps, such as deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance, which can add up quickly. Additionally, there's no cap on out-of-pocket expenses. This is where supplemental coverage, like Medigap, becomes essential.

How Medigap Enhances Your Medicare Coverage

Original Medicare

The coverage provided by Original Medicare (Parts A and B) can often leave extensive costs that aggregate from copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles.

Bridging the Gap

Medigap, also known as Medical supplement, provides many different plans, that work towards filling the "gaps" of what is not covered by Original Medicare, so as to fight against the rising cost of healthcare and unexpected medical bills.

Benefits

To name a few, some of the good in a medigap plan stems from more predictable healthcare costs, freedom in choosing any hospitls in the US that accepts Medicare, and standarized plans across insurers for easy comparisons.
